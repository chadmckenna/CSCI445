Hiking in Golden Gate Canyon

Over 35 miles of hiking trails in the park offer pleasures and challenges for everyone. The twelve trails at Golden Gate Canyon are each named after an animal and marked with the animal's footprint.

Trailhead parking areas are easily accessible from the main roads in the park.

Mountain bikes and horses are permitted in the park on multiple-use trails. Ample parking space for horse trailers is provided at the Nott Creek trailhead located near the Red Barn Group Picnic Area and at Kriley Overlook above Kriley Pond.

Nature at Golden Gate State Park

Golden Gate State Park provides a home for a great number and variety of wildlife, providing the perfect place for wildlife watching and wildlife photography. Among the larger animals are moose, bighorn sheep, black bear, mule deer and elk. Smaller mammals include the snowshoe hare, coyote, bobcat, beaver, weasel, marten, fox, marmot, and porcupine. Common birds include Blue Grouse, Ptarmigan, Golden Eagle, Bald Eagle, Steller’s Jay, Gray Jay, Chickadee, Rosy Finch, and several species of waterfowl. Fish species include brook trout, cutthroat trout, brown trout, rainbow trout and arctic grayling. Cabins and Yurts

There are five camper cabins and two yurts in Reverend's Ridge campground. They accommodate a maximum of six people each. Call 1-800-678-2267 or (303)470-1144 for reservations. A new guest house, the Harmsen Ranch Guest House and corral, is open for use.

Campgrounds

There are 132 campsites in two campgrounds: Reverend’s Ridge and Aspen Meadow. Reverend’s Ridge offers 38 tent sites and 59 sites with electrical hook-ups. Facilities at Reverend’s Ridge include flush toilets, shower and laundry facilities and a dump station (Some facilities ONLY available during warm weather months). Aspen Meadow offers 35 tent sites and vault toilets (Closed in winter). Golden Gate also offers 20 backcountry tent sites and four backcountry shelters. Rifleman Phillips Group Campground (closed in the winter) can accommodate up to 75 people. It is located in a 50-year-old stand of lodge pole pine and is restricted to tents only For reservation information, call (303) 582-3707.

Conference Rooms

The Columbine Meeting Room can accommodate 24 people and is available for classes, conferences and meetings. Call (303) 582-3707 for reservations.

Dump Station

A dump station (closed in winter) is available at the Reverends' Ridge Campground with the purchase of an $20 electrical camping permit. A vehicle pass is also required.

Group Campground

Rifleman Phillips Group Campground is available for groups up to 75 people. This campground is for tent campers only. For more information and reservations please call 303-582-3707.

Group Picnic Area

The Red Barn Group Picnic Area can accommodate up to 150 people. Call (303) 582-3707 for reservations. Visit the "Group Facilities" link on the left of Golden Gate Canyon's main page for more information.
